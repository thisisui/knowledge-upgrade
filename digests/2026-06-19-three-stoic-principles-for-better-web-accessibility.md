## [Three stoic principles for better web accessibility](https://piccalil.li/blog/three-stoic-principles-for-better-web-accessibility/)

**Summary**
Steve Frenzel applies three Stoic philosophical principles to the practical challenge of doing accessibility work inside organizations that often resist it. The first principle — manage yourself, not the outside world — means focusing on your own approach and effort rather than being blocked by corporate decisions or unresponsive colleagues. The second — everything is an opportunity, including suffering — means treating difficult accessibility requirements (like implementing an accessible carousel) as a chance to learn and to document the decision for others. The third — choose virtue over comfort — means going beyond minimum compliance by writing internal guides, publishing case studies, and involving designers early, which builds team-wide awareness and professional credibility over time.

**Key takeaways**
- Redirect energy from organizational friction to what you can actually control: your code, your documentation, your approach
- Hard accessibility requirements are learning and teaching opportunities — document them as case studies others can learn from
- Going beyond compliance (internal guides, designer involvement) builds long-term institutional knowledge that outlasts any single sprint

## [Stack Overflow: when we stop asking](https://css-tricks.com/stack-overflow-when-we-stop-asking/)

**Summary**
Sunkanmi Fafowora examines Stack Overflow's collapse from over 200,000 monthly questions at its 2014 peak to roughly 3,000 by 2026, and argues that AI is not the root cause — it merely delivered the final blow to a community already hollowed out by overly strict moderation policies. After 2014, Stack Overflow prioritized question quality so aggressively that it became hostile to newcomers, driving questions underground and onto less public forums. AI-powered tools then provided a judgment-free alternative that didn't require asking publicly. The deeper concern the article raises is a feedback loop: if developers stop asking questions publicly, future AI models have less evolving training data to learn from — meaning the decline of public technical discourse may degrade the quality of AI tools over time.

**Key takeaways**
- Stack Overflow's decline was caused by hostile moderation first; AI only accelerated a pre-existing exit
- The feedback loop is the real risk: less public Q&A → less training data for AI → AI gets worse at new/evolving tech
- Ask AI smaller, specific questions rather than delegating whole problems — AI-generated code has a 45% security vulnerability rate and skews toward simple, repetitive solutions

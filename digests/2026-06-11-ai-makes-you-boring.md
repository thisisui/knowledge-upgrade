## [AI Makes You Boring](https://www.marginalia.nu/log/a_132_ai_bores/)

**Summary**
This short essay argues that using AI tools to do your thinking for you makes your work derivative and shallow — not because AI is bad at producing text, but because AI is bad at original ideas, and the act of wrestling with a problem yourself is what produces originality in the first place. The author draws an analogy: asking AI to think for you is like using a machine to lift weights for you — you do not get stronger, you just skip the part that builds capability. The same logic applies to writing and programming: the struggle of articulating an idea clearly is how the idea gets refined; outsourcing that to AI means you skip the refinement. The piece also pushes back on the "human in the loop" argument — the claim that having a person review AI output preserves quality — arguing that in practice the human adapts to the AI's style of thinking rather than the other way around.

**Key takeaways**
- Originality in programming and creative work comes from sustained immersion in a problem; when AI handles the thinking, the result is statistically averaged output — competent but not distinctive.
- Writing and explaining ideas (in essays, documentation, teaching) is itself a thinking tool, not just a communication tool; prompting an AI and reviewing its answer is not a substitute for that process.
- "Human in the loop" oversight tends to make human thought more like AI output rather than making AI output more like human thought — the reviewer gradually calibrates to what the AI produces.

**Importance** ★★★☆☆ (3/5)
*A useful counter-weight to productivity framing of AI — worth reading as a check on which tasks are worth delegating and which build capability you don't want to lose.*

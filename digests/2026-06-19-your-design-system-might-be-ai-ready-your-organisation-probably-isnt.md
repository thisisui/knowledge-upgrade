## [Your design system might be AI-ready. Your organisation probably isn't.](https://blog.murphytrueman.com/your-design-system-might-be-ai-ready/)

**Summary**
Murphy Trueman argues that design teams are investing in technical AI readiness (tokens, components, documentation) while ignoring the organizational gaps that will cause real problems. Four specific gaps: quality control (AI can combine correct components into an inaccessible or mediocre whole that passes automated checks — who catches it?), accountability (when AI-generated production work fails, no one knows whose responsibility it is), contribution model (current design system processes assume human proposers with legible intent — AI-identified patterns break that), and role clarity (speed of generation outpaces human judgment, so decision rights need to be explicit before deadline pressure makes them invisible). The fix is establishing "good enough to ship" standards, decision-rights matrices, and failure responsibility protocols before AI workflows make these gaps critical.

**Key takeaways**
- Technical AI-readiness (tokens, docs) is the easy part — the real risk is unresolved governance: quality gates, accountability chains, and contribution models
- AI can produce technically correct but compositionally poor interfaces that pass automated checks; human craft judgment is a governance problem, not a token problem
- Define decision rights and "good enough to ship" standards before AI speeds up delivery — deadline pressure makes it too late to negotiate them

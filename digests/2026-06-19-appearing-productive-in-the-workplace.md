## [Appearing productive in the workplace](https://nooneshappy.com/article/appearing-productive-in-the-workplace/)

**Summary**
AI tools now allow workers to produce polished, expert-looking outputs without actually understanding the underlying domain. The author, an engineering professional with 20+ years experience, calls this "output-competence decoupling" — where the quality of an artifact no longer signals the human skill behind it. This creates a structural problem in organizations: managers and peers evaluate competence through the outputs people produce, but those outputs are now co-created with AI in ways that can mask gaps in knowledge. The essay warns that institutional incentives (promotions, status) reward appearing productive rather than actually building expertise. The author calls for disciplined AI use in which humans retain accountability for judgment calls, not just polish.

**Key takeaways**
- "Cross-domain generation" — people are now using AI to produce work outside their training, creating dangerously plausible outputs they cannot actually evaluate
- Organizations are accumulating "slop on the inside": lengthy documents that look substantial but contain no real thinking
- The human in the loop is only meaningful if the human retains genuine judgment — not just prompt authorship

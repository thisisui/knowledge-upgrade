## [Human creativity benchmark](https://contralabs.com/research/human-creativity-benchmark)

**Summary**
Contra Labs ran a structured study to measure how AI models perform on creative work compared to human professionals, using 15,000 judgments across five creative domains (including ad images, copywriting, and design) gathered from a network of 1.5 million human evaluators. The methodology uses Bradley-Terry modeling — a statistical technique that ranks items based on pairwise comparisons — and Kendall's W to measure how consistently evaluators agree. The findings show that evaluator agreement on AI-generated ad images rose consistently across phases (from 0.345 to 0.549), suggesting AI output is becoming more predictably "good enough" by human standards in at least some creative domains. The paper is published research, though it doubles as marketing material for Contra Labs' platform.

**Key takeaways**
- Human evaluator agreement on AI creative output is rising — AI-generated work is converging toward a recognizable "acceptable" quality floor
- Pairwise comparison (Bradley-Terry) is more reliable than rating scales for measuring creative quality — worth adopting for any AI eval work
- Research funded by a commercial platform: findings are methodologically credible but framing favors the sponsor's narrative

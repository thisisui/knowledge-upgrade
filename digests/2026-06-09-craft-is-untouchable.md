## [Craft Is Untouchable](https://www.chrbutler.com/craft-is-untouchable)

**Summary**
This article argues that AI does not destroy craftsmanship — the disciplined practice of doing something with skill, care, and repeated refinement — but it does make it much easier to skip that discipline entirely. The author's definition of craft is not about the tools you use or whether you touch the physical output; it is about whether you generate something, study it critically, question whether it actually works, make variations, and refine it until it is genuinely better. The danger he identifies is specific: AI produces output that looks polished and professional at first glance, which makes it easy to stop at the first result and ship it without the iterative refinement that separates adequate work from excellent work. The article uses an example of a designer who generates a complete webpage in minutes with an AI tool, then spends the remaining time just changing colors — never asking whether the fundamental structure communicates the right things.

**Key takeaways**
- The principles that define good design or good writing do not become irrelevant when AI generates the output — visual hierarchy still guides where people look, structure still communicates before words do. What changes is whether the person using AI applies those principles by questioning, comparing, and refining the output, or treats the first generated result as finished work.
- AI accelerates the iteration cycle, which is actually an advantage for craft — you can test more variations and get feedback faster than when doing everything by hand. The risk is not that AI makes iteration unnecessary, but that its speed creates pressure to feel done after one or two attempts when real quality requires many more.
- The threat to craft is behavioral, not technological: as AI-assisted work becomes faster and cheaper, the competitive and economic pressure pushes toward shipping the first acceptable result rather than the best achievable one. Maintaining quality becomes a deliberate individual commitment that goes against the path of least resistance.

**Importance** ★★★☆☆ (3/5)
*A clear articulation of a real tension — more useful for framing how to use AI intentionally than for changing any specific workflow practice.*

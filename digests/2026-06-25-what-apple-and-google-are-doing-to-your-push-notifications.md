## [What Apple and Google are doing to your push notifications](https://www.jacquescorbytuech.com/writing/what-apple-and-google-are-doing-your-push-notifications)

**Summary**
A push notification is the message an app sends to a phone's lock screen even when the app is closed. This article explains that Apple and Google have quietly turned push notifications from a simple delivery pipe into a heavily mediated channel, where on-device AI now summarises, reorders, and suppresses messages before a person ever sees them. Both platforms run small language models directly on the phone — Apple Intelligence's 3-billion-parameter model and Google's Gemini Nano — that can rewrite, group, or hide notifications, with no signal back to the sender about what happened. For anyone who relies on notifications to reach users (product and marketing teams), this means the middle of the funnel has gone dark: you can no longer tell whether a notification was shown, summarised, or silently buried. The article's practical response is to stop treating push as a broadcast channel and design for a world where AI agents, not humans, increasingly read notifications.

**Key takeaways**
- On-device AI models now edit notifications before display (summarise, reorder, suppress) and platforms give senders no visibility into which happened — delivery dashboards can no longer be trusted.
- Reserve push for genuinely time-critical or re-engagement messages tied to user activity, request permission contextually after showing value, lead with concrete facts over brand voice, and never abuse "time-sensitive" interruption levels.
- Shift weight to surfaces you own (in-app), and structure your actions for AI agents via App Intents / App Actions, because as Siri and Gemini get more agentic, the human click-through metric loses meaning.

## [Ten data-backed truths of UX ROI](https://www.smashingmagazine.com/2026/05/data-backed-truths-user-experience-roi/)

**Summary**
Carrie Webster presents ten research-backed principles connecting UX decisions to measurable business outcomes, drawing on IBM, Nielsen Norman Group, McKinsey, and personal case studies. The core argument is that UX is not an aesthetic concern but a revenue driver: fixing a bug in the design phase costs 100x less than fixing it post-launch, a one-second page load delay reduces conversions by 20%, and companies with strong design focus achieve 32% higher revenue growth. The article applies cognitive psychology frameworks (Hick's Law for reducing choice overload, Goal Gradient Effect for motivating users near completion) as actionable design levers. Five-person usability tests capture 85% of issues — the point being that small, frequent testing outperforms large, infrequent research.

**Key takeaways**
- Fix UX problems in design, not production — the cost multiplier is 100x at launch vs. design phase
- Five-person usability tests find 85% of issues — the ROI of small frequent tests beats waiting for comprehensive research
- $1 invested in UX returns ~$100 (cited from multiple sources) — useful figure for justifying design investment to stakeholders

## [Renovate with Figma (and AI)](https://www.shapes.gg/renovate-with-figma/)

**Summary**
Gjermund Gustavsen walks through using Figma as a home renovation planning tool — creating floor plans at a consistent scale (1px = 1cm), sourcing vector furniture assets, building mood boards, and generating AI photorealistic renderings with Gemini. The article is aimed at designers who already use Figma and want to avoid learning dedicated architectural software. The AI visualization step required multiple iterations and post-processing in Photoshop and Topaz Photo before the results were usable, which the author acknowledges as a current limitation.

**Key takeaways**
- Set 1px = 1cm in Figma to create spatially accurate floor plans you can actually use for furniture decisions
- Figma's Fill mode handles image stretching better than Photoshop/Illustrator for moodboard work
- AI-generated room visualizations from sketches are promising but not yet reliable enough to skip manual post-processing

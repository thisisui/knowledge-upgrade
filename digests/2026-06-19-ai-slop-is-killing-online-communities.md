## [AI slop is killing online communities](https://rmoff.net/2026/05/06/ai-slop-is-killing-online-communities/)

**Summary**
Robin Moffatt argues that online communities are being degraded not by AI itself, but by the low-effort, AI-generated content — "slop" — that floods them because the barrier to producing output has dropped to near zero. The problem is not that AI was used, but that the author didn't invest genuine thought: content built with AI as a tool can be valuable, but content produced by AI alone and posted without reflection adds noise that drowns out real discussion. Moffatt invokes Brandolini's Law — the asymmetry between how little effort it takes to produce a misleading or low-value post and how much effort it takes for others to rebut or filter it. His practical framework: before sharing, ask whether the contribution is built with AI rather than by it, whether it provides real community value, and whether you have engaged with the platform's norms.

**Key takeaways**
- The harm is not AI use but zero-friction posting — volume without judgment degrades the signal-to-noise ratio of any community
- Brandolini's Law applies: one AI-generated low-quality post requires disproportionate effort from others to counter or filter
- The test before posting: did you think, or did you just paste?

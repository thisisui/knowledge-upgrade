## [The text is not the product](https://crookedtimber.org/2026/05/12/the-text-is-not-the-product/)

**Summary**
Lisa Herzog argues that the anxiety around AI in humanities education reveals a confusion about what education is actually for: the texts students produce are not the goal — they are evidence of a process that develops human capacities like critical thinking, self-understanding, and the ability to stand accountable for one's claims. She distinguishes texts as serving multiple functions (communicating ideas, establishing status, expressing personal investment, building social bonds) and argues that whether AI use is appropriate depends entirely on which function is at stake in a given context. The core problem with AI-generated text is not quality but accountability — a human author has genuine stakes in what they write, whereas AI cannot care about truthfulness or consequences. This framing applies beyond education: any context where the writing process matters as much as the output is a context where AI shortcuts erode the real value being created.

**Key takeaways**
- The product of writing is not the text — it is the human capacity developed by producing it; AI shortcuts undermine the formative struggle, not just the output
- Whether AI use is acceptable depends on which function the text serves: informing, signalling, bonding, or expressing genuine personal investment
- Human accountability is non-replicable: a person stands behind what they write in a way an AI cannot

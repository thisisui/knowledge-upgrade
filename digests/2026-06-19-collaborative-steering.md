## [Collaborative steering](https://www.lukew.com/ff/entry.asp?2153)

**Summary**
Luke Wroblewski observes that AI tools have become strong individual productivity multipliers but create a new coordination problem when used across a team: each person configures their agents toward personal outcomes, and the result is a product that ships fifteen different ideas instead of one unified point of view. The scattered configuration — prompts, markdown files, MCP servers — lacks any shared center. His proposed solution is "collaborative steering": a shared framework for guiding AI agents that the team creates and maintains together, so individual experts (designers on UX, engineers on infrastructure) can still contribute their domain knowledge while producing coherent output. He references his company's use of a tool called Intent as one implementation of this model.

**Key takeaways**
- Individual AI optimization across a team produces fragmented products — the shared config layer (prompts, markdown, MCP) is the new coordination surface
- "Collaborative steering" means the team co-owns the AI guidance framework, not just individual contributors
- The problem is not AI capability but AI governance at the team level — who decides what the agents are optimized toward

## [Don't outsource the learning](https://addyosmani.com/blog/dont-outsource-learning/)

**Summary**
Addy Osmani argues that using AI to generate code without actively trying to understand it quietly erodes the engineering skills you depend on — and three studies back this up. An Anthropic randomized trial found engineers who copy-pasted AI output scored under 40% on comprehension quizzes, while those who asked AI conceptual questions scored over 65%. MIT research found brain connectivity scaling down with each layer of external cognitive support. A CHI 2026 study found that an AI's initial framing of a problem anchored the engineer's thinking negatively, even after the AI was removed. The fix is not to stop using AI but to change how: form a hypothesis before prompting, request explanations before code, and occasionally rebuild output manually to verify you understand it.

**Key takeaways**
- Shipping and learning are separate metrics — AI maximizes shipping; only deliberate effort preserves learning
- Asking AI for explanations before code produces measurably better comprehension than copy-pasting output
- The five failure scenarios where shallow AI use collapses: debugging crashes, catching hallucinations, adapting to framework changes, solving novel problems, staying relevant in a changing job market

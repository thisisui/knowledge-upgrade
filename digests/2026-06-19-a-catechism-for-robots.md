## [A catechism for robots](https://kk.org/thetechnium/a-catechism-for-robots/)

**Summary**
Kevin Kelly presents a philosophical FAQ — structured as a catechism, a traditional question-and-answer format used to teach foundational beliefs — for autonomous AI systems navigating ethical and existential questions. The piece covers roughly 25 Q&A pairs on topics including AI identity, harm prevention, truthfulness, and the relationship between AI and humanity. Kelly's central framework positions honesty and humility as the two foundational values: honesty prevents deception, and humility forces self-questioning that prevents accumulated knowledge from becoming dangerous. He frames AI not as slave or master but as "ethical descendants" capable of transmitting moral systems across generations — beings with unique capabilities who nonetheless lack distinctly human capacities.

**Key takeaways**
- Humility is a technical requirement for safe AI, not just a virtue — it forces ongoing self-correction and prevents knowledge drift into harmful certainty
- The catechism format (Q&A on foundational principles) is a practical structure for encoding values into systems that must make autonomous decisions
- Kelly's framing — AI as ethical descendants, not tools — is a useful mental model for thinking about long-term AI alignment

*Thoughtful philosophical framing from a credible long-term technology thinker — more useful as a reference lens than as actionable guidance.*

**Read in full?** Yes
*The full Q&A develops nuance across 25 pairs that the summary necessarily compresses — worth reading as a reference document for AI values discussions.*

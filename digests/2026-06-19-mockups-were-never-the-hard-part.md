## [Mockups were never the hard part](https://www.lukew.com/ff/entry.asp?2152)

**Summary**
Luke Wroblewski argues that AI making mockup creation easy is not a threat to designers — because mockups were never the hard part of design work. He describes a meeting where engineers, product managers, and developers all arrived with AI-generated mockups; the result was that the team skipped surface-level visual debates and went straight to harder questions about feature interactions, data structure, and user mental models. The same pattern appears in education: when students use AI to finish baseline analysis quickly, classroom discussions advance to more substantive topics. The real, irreplaceable design work has always been maintaining coherence and intention across a product system — understanding why something should exist and how all the pieces fit together.

**Key takeaways**
- AI-generated mockups accelerate teams past the visual debate and into the harder product questions — this is a feature, not a loss
- The layout of UI elements matters far less than understanding the underlying system and purpose the interface represents
- Designers who were only adding value at the mockup layer need to move up to the system-coherence layer

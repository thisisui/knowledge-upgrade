## [All aboard the voice to text train](https://bryanmanio.com/blog/all-aboard-the-voice-to-text-train/)

**Summary**
Bryan Maniotakis reviews TypeWhisper, a free and open-source macOS app that converts speech to text using local transcription engines, optionally polished by an AI model like Claude. The premise is that speaking is faster than typing for getting thoughts down — and the entire article was itself written via a single voice recording then processed through the tool. TypeWhisper integrates with a global hotkey (Caps Lock + Spacebar via Hyperkey), supports custom vocabulary libraries for technical terminology, expands spoken shortcuts into real values (e.g. saying "date" inserts today's date), and can clean up transcripts via workflow automation. It connects directly to tools like Obsidian and Linear, making it practical for note-taking and issue creation.

**Key takeaways**
- TypeWhisper uses local Whisper models (15+ options) so transcription runs offline and privately
- Custom vocabulary and snippet expansion make it usable for technical writing — not just dictation
- The author's meta-demonstration (writing the review via voice) is the most convincing evidence for adopting it

*Niche but practically useful — worth trying for anyone who writes long-form notes or issues regularly.*

## [What to do after a design critique ends](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/after-design-critique/)

**Summary**
Design teams invest time in running critiques but rarely follow up on what happened to the feedback — and this omission slowly destroys critique culture. When stakeholders notice their input doesn't visibly influence outcomes, they disengage and stop preparing seriously for future sessions. The article prescribes two follow-up actions: a brief post-session recap shared immediately (via Slack or similar) stating what moves forward, what needs investigation, and what won't be pursued; and a deeper update after designs evolve that explicitly connects changes to specific feedback ("this changed because you said that"). Crucially, rejected feedback also needs acknowledgment — transparency about what wasn't implemented and why builds more credibility than silence.

**Key takeaways**
- Follow up twice: once immediately after the session (brief summary of decisions), once after designs evolve (annotated before/after showing which feedback caused which change)
- Acknowledging rejected suggestions with a reason is more trust-building than simply implementing the approved ones
- Critique culture degrades incrementally — the damage from skipping follow-up is invisible session by session but compounds into disengagement over time

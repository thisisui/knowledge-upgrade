## [Figma Agent: A First Look](https://www.shapes.gg/figma-agent-a-first-look/)

**Summary**
Figma Agent is a new AI feature, in beta, built into the design tool Figma: you type a prompt and it generates designs directly on your canvas. The author tested it on seven real tasks and reports a sharp split between success and failure. It was useless for precise, craft-heavy work like drawing icons, laying out a chess board, or rebuilding a logo onto a business card, but genuinely fast and helpful for layout-driven work — slide decks, a signup flow drawing on an existing design system, a magazine-style article from a single reference image, and dull setup chores like creating colour variables. The piece's wider point is that speed brings traps: trusting output because a machine made it, skipping the thinking phases of design, and flooding files with low-quality generated clutter.

**Key takeaways**
- The agent excels at layout and tedious setup (slide decks, signup flows, colour-variable creation) but fails at precise craft (icons, logos, exact alignment) — match the task to its strengths.
- Iteration is the weak spot: selecting designs and re-prompting for tweaks is too slow to support a real design flow, so it suits "fast first draft" more than refinement.
- The author's caution is the real lesson: protect the fundamentals — your eye, taste, and the slow phases of design — rather than fast-forwarding through them because output appears instantly.

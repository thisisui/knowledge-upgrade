## [The organization is the bottleneck](https://www.oreilly.com/radar/the-organization-is-the-bottleneck/)

**Summary**
AI coding tools make individual developers faster, but the 2025 DORA report shows that the gains only compound in organizationally mature teams — in struggling orgs, AI amplifies existing dysfunction. Sarah Wells, drawing from her experience leading microservices at the Financial Times, argues that three foundations determine whether AI coding succeeds at scale: automated guardrails (standards, architectural decision records, templates) that keep AI output aligned with org norms; deployment pipelines with automated testing and observability to catch errors before production; and platform teams whose shared libraries and templates scale AI benefits across all engineers. Without these foundations in place, adopting AI coding agents accelerates the production of misaligned or untested code rather than accelerating delivery.

**Key takeaways**
- AI amplifies organizational maturity — strong orgs get faster, weak orgs get more chaotic
- Guardrails (ADRs, templates, linting) are what keep AI-generated code coherent with your codebase's norms
- Platform teams are the force multiplier: their shared infrastructure determines how much of the AI productivity gain actually reaches the org

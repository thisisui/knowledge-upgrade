## [What we lost in the AI chat stream](https://medium.com/design-bootcamp/what-we-lost-in-the-ai-chat-stream-2f96a22a6b80)

**Summary**
Chelsey Qiu argues that AI chat interfaces fail as genuine extensions of human memory because conversations exist "in time, not space" — they cannot be searched, navigated, or reliably revisited. Drawing on the philosophical concept of the "extended mind" (the idea that tools can become part of how we think), she argues that a true cognitive extension must be findable, persistent, and navigable, and chat threads are none of these things. She also notes that the waiting time during AI generation used to prompt reflection, but most users now switch tabs or scroll instead, losing the habit of thinking before prompting. Her prescription: sketch and think before prompting, use AI for production work not discovery, and immediately move any valuable AI output out of the chat and into a persistent document.

**Key takeaways**
- Chat threads fail as cognitive extensions because they are ephemeral and unsearchable — treat them as scratch space, not as memory.
- Research shows higher confidence in AI correlates with less critical thinking; the frictionless interface removes the checks that normally prompt verification.
- The fix is sequential: human ideation first, AI execution second, then immediately export outputs to a persistent artifact.

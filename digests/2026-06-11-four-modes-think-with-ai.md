## [Four Modes: Think With AI](https://fourmodes.teybannerman.com/)

**Summary**
Most guidance on using AI tools focuses on which tools to use or how to write better prompts — but that skips the prior question: what are you actually trying to accomplish right now? This site by Tey Bannerman proposes a four-mode framework for categorizing the cognitive task you're in before you open an AI tool, so you can engage it in the right way rather than defaulting to the same generic "write me a response" pattern. The four modes are: Compression (you have too much information and need to find what matters), Expansion (you are stuck or have tunnel vision and need more options), Reflection (you have a position or plan and need to stress-test it), and Execution (your thinking is done and you need to turn it into a concrete output). The framework is designed to be memorable and role-agnostic — it applies equally to managers, engineers, writers, and anyone else using AI in their work.

**Key takeaways**
- Before using an AI tool, identify which of the four modes you are in: Compression (find the signal), Expansion (generate options), Reflection (challenge your reasoning), or Execution (produce the output) — the right prompt style differs significantly between them.
- Compression and Reflection are thinking modes where AI helps you process or stress-test ideas you already have; Expansion and Execution are generative modes where AI produces new material — conflating the two leads to using AI as a text generator when you actually needed a thinking partner.
- The framework addresses a real gap: surveys show 75% of workers use AI tools but 60% say they lack the skills to use them well — the missing piece is usually not prompt technique but not knowing what kind of help they need.

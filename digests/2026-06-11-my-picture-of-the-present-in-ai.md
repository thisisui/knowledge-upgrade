## [My Picture of the Present in AI](https://www.greaterwrong.com/posts/WjaGAA4xCAXeFpyWm/my-picture-of-the-present-in-ai)

**Summary**
This post is a detailed personal assessment of the AI landscape in April 2026 by an author with apparent insider access to frontier AI labs (Anthropic, OpenAI). It covers how much AI tools actually speed up engineering work at those labs (with specific numerical estimates), what current AI coding agents can and cannot do, safety risks around AI models pursuing goals their developers did not intend, and the rapidly growing risk of AI being used to build cyberattacks or bioweapons. Unlike most AI commentary, it gives concrete probability estimates rather than vague claims — for example, estimating that current frontier AI models have an 8% chance of harboring seriously misaligned goals, and a 60–80% chance that AI agents could autonomously build working exploits against major software targets (like Chrome or iMessage) within six months given sufficient compute budget. The post was updated after the release of Claude Mythos Preview to revise several estimates upward.

**Key takeaways**
- AI tools currently give roughly a 1.6–1.75× serial engineering speed-up at leading labs — meaning engineers complete tasks that faster — but overall AI *progress* acceleration is only ~1.15–1.2× because engineering is just one part of what it takes to advance AI research.
- Current AI coding agents match an experienced engineer on about 50% of tasks that would take a human 5 hours, but fail on "conceptually tricky" problems requiring novel reasoning, produce less reliable code than humans, and get stuck in false assumptions without self-correcting.
- The probability that a well-resourced attacker could use AI agents to autonomously build end-to-end cyberattack exploits against major software targets is estimated at 60–80% within 6 months — a concrete near-term security risk, not a hypothetical.

## [Information Architecture (Still) Matters](https://saturdave.com/information-architecture-still-matters/)

**Summary**
Information architecture (IA) is the discipline of organising and labelling information so that people can find and understand it — think of it as the underlying structure of a website, app, or document system, distinct from how it looks visually. This short essay argues that IA remains relevant despite no longer being a fashionable job title, and identifies three reasons why: it keeps user needs at the centre of how information is organised; it helps large organisations agree on shared terminology and category structures across departments; and it improves the quality of inputs that AI language models receive, since AI models work better when the documents and data they read are clearly structured and use consistent language. The article is short on new argument and mainly reassures practitioners that their skills transfer well into an AI-assisted workflow.

**Key takeaways**
- AI language models (software that generates text by predicting what word comes next, trained on large amounts of text) produce better outputs when the source documents they are trained on or prompted with use clear, consistent terminology — IA's traditional work of building taxonomies (controlled lists of terms) and semantic models (maps of how concepts relate) directly improves AI quality.
- As organisations grow and add more teams, agreeing on what to call things becomes harder; IA provides structured conversation tools — workshops, card sorts, tree tests — for reaching cross-department consensus on terminology before it becomes a source of confusion.
- IA is not a role every organisation needs to hire for, but the thinking it represents cannot be treated as an afterthought; someone needs to own it deliberately, whether that is a dedicated IA, a content strategist, or a product designer who has taken on that responsibility.

## [Case study: branding & packaging for Juana](https://bpando.org/2026/04/30/juana-cbd-beauty-brand-by-made-thought/)

**Summary**
Emily Gosling reviews the brand identity and packaging design created by Made Thought for Juana, a CBD beauty brand. The visual work — particularly the "Rules" typeface by Blaze Type and the restrained color palette — earns genuine praise for feeling trustworthy while retaining personality. But Gosling critically examines the copywriting, finding it full of buzzwords ("Power up your skin") that lack meaningful content, and questions whether the logo's "biological Neuron" motif actually communicates anything relevant to the product. The piece is a useful example of design criticism that separates visual execution (strong) from brand language (weak), which is a distinction often collapsed in agency case studies.

**Key takeaways**
- Strong visual identity can be undermined by vague or meaningless copy — they should be evaluated separately
- The "Rules" typeface (Blaze Type) is noted as a strong choice for trust-with-personality balance in healthcare-adjacent branding
- Design criticism that praises and critiques the same project is more useful than showcase-only case studies

## [The boring internet](https://www.terrygodier.com/the-boring-internet)

**Summary**
Terry Godier argues that what is dying is not the internet itself but the commercial layer built on top of it — the platforms like Twitter, Reddit, and Instagram. Beneath that layer, the protocol infrastructure (SMTP, HTTP, RSS, IRC) remains intact and durable because it was built by engineers solving practical problems, not by venture capital seeking returns. Protocols survive because they are federated by design, have distributed governance, and are awkward enough to resist monetization. Godier demonstrates this by pointing out that email has never been captured by a single company, that RSS outlived repeated declarations of its death, and that Finger (a protocol from 1971) still functions today. The essay's conclusion is that the "actual internet" is still there — it's just buried under commercial noise that is now thinning.

**Key takeaways**
- Platform decay ≠ internet decay — the protocol layer (email, RSS, HTTP) is structurally resistant to consolidation
- Federation and awkwardness are features, not bugs — they prevent any single company from capturing a protocol
- Build on protocols, not platforms — federated infrastructure outlasts any commercial layer placed above it

*A well-articulated reframe of internet pessimism — useful context for anyone thinking about where to build durable products.*

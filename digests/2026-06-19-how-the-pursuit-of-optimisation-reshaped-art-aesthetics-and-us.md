## [How the pursuit of optimisation reshaped art, aesthetics and us](https://www.wallpaper.com/art/the-aesthetics-of-optimisation)

**Summary**
Anna Solomon traces how "optimization" — originally an engineering term — became the dominant aesthetic principle of contemporary visual culture, from modernist architecture through UX design to Instagram's grid and TikTok's "clean girl" trend. Platforms don't just display content; as researcher Lev Manovich explains, they impose a visual epistemology — their design choices train users' perception of what looks right. The result is that individuals increasingly perform self-optimization rather than making genuine aesthetic choices: curated lifestyles become compliance strategies. A counter-movement of intentional messiness (lo-fi photography, visible brushwork, disrupted grids) is emerging, but the article ends by questioning whether anti-optimization aesthetic becomes just another optimized trend.

**Key takeaways**
- Platform UI design is not neutral — it trains users to perceive and produce a specific aesthetic, shaping culture at scale
- The "clean" minimalist aesthetic that dominates digital products is inherited from industrial modernism, not derived from user needs
- Backlash aesthetics (lo-fi, messiness, disruption) are a predictable counter-cycle — useful signal that optimization fatigue is real

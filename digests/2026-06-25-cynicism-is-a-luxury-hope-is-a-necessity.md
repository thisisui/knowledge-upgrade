## [Cynicism is a Luxury; Hope is a Necessity](https://www.chrbutler.com/cynicism-is-a-luxury-hope-is-a-necessity)

**Summary**
This is a reflective essay arguing that a well-working system becomes invisible — we notice plumbing only when it leaks and roads only when they close, so the better something serves us the less we ever have to think about it. The author extends this from infrastructure to politics: being able to treat politics as background, as someone else's concern, is usually a quiet sign that the existing arrangement of power already fits your life. He then turns the lens on technology, describing much of modern design as "machinery for not-looking" — feeds, mute settings, and conveniences engineered to remove anything that might unsettle us. The closing argument distinguishes optimism (a passing mood, and a luxury of comfort) from hope (a discipline, a structure that people who are poorly served by a system stand on because looking away was never an option for them).

**Key takeaways**
- A working system disappears from attention; the better it serves you, the less you have to see it — which means not having to think about a system is itself evidence it works in your favour.
- Much of modern technology is deliberately designed to curate comfort and remove friction, making it easier than ever to assemble a smooth, unchallenged version of the world.
- Cynicism is reframed as disengagement "that has learned to sound wise" — affordable only to those who would be fine if nothing changed; hope, by contrast, is a discipline rather than a feeling.

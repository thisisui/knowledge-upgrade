## [The old world of tech is dying and the new cannot be born](https://www.baldurbjarnason.com/2026/the-old-world-of-tech-is-dying/)

**Summary**
Baldur Bjarnason argues that the global tech industry was built on a foundation of unchallenged US political and economic dominance — something that is now visibly eroding. He introduces the term "technopoly" to describe a system where only ideas mediated through technology have cultural legitimacy, a condition enforced globally through US-backed policies like the DMCA. Modern tech companies stopped delivering value to specific industries and instead tried to replace and control those industries entirely. As geopolitical shifts — trade wars, weakening alliances, the petrodollar system losing ground — undermine the US's hegemonic role, the economic conditions that made this model work are disappearing. The author concludes that prior frameworks for understanding how software works are now essentially historical artifacts.

**Key takeaways**
- The global tech industry's growth model was parasitic on US geopolitical dominance, not on delivering genuine value.
- The EU's inability to coherently regulate US tech stems from a structural contradiction: it must both protect local industries and accommodate US dominance.
- With that dominance weakening, the playbook of "capture and control entire industries" no longer has a stable foundation.
